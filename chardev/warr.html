S neaking Races

Races: Dark-elf, Elf, Halfling, Goblin, Nekojin

Pro's Vs Con's: If you want to add a little excitement and a challenge to your warrior make them a sneaker. Honestly they won't sneak all that great till they start to reach higher levels. In the end, however, they make some of the bester exploring warriors vs the non-sneakers. Imagine being able to move though any terrian without detection and out of harms way. And those Back stabs start to come in handy around level 30. Best choices are Dark-elf, lower exp table, Elf, or Neokjin. You will be laughed at in the arena and take a little longer to level than the non sneakers but once you get to level 25 then you will know why you picked a sneaker vs a non sneaker. Imgine getting even with a Bs for 200+.

Making a Sneaker
Stat Suggestions (In order of raise)

  • Health = get this to 80 first
  • Agility = raise it 10-20, should be easy to to adds little stealth and dodge
  • Strength = get to 80, hardest stat of sneakers to raise besides health but most important, improves stealth and bs, besides being able to carry more armour
  • Intel = raise to 50, improves stealth, and crits
  • Charm = raise to 50, improves stealth, crits and dodge
  • Health = max this next, get lots of hps, Mwhahaha!!!
  • Agility = raise to 100, swing more and dodge more, also improve stealth tremendously
  • Strength = max it, hit harder and carry more armour
  • Intel = raise to 100, makes you start crit-ing everwhere and improves stealth
  • Agility = max this
  • Charm = raise to 90, improves stealth, and you'll notice you'll get hit much less
  • Wisdom = raise this only after every stat is at least 80 not important to warriors besides Magic Resistance

N on Sneaking Races

Races: Human, Dwarf, Gnome, Half-elf, Half-Orc, Half-Ogre, Kang, and Guant

Pro's Vs Con's: Over all warriors that don't sneak should carry lots of armour to make up for the lack of stealth. They usually on the average have more hps than their sneaking counter parts and are stronger with higher AC. They are some of the easiest characters to build up in the beginning but make for a little bit of boredom in higher lvls. Best choices are Dwarf, Kang and Half-Orc. Having to live without lanterns, is it really worth it? You decide.
