Sneaking Races

Races: Elf, nekojin, Dark-elf

Pro's Vs Con's: Since rangers are sneakers by default, why not just keep them that way with a sneaking class. They start with higher stealth and their bs's are usually harder hitting.

Making a Sneaker
Stat Suggestions (In order of raise)

  • Strength = 60 (for bigger weapons (like katana)
  • Agility = Get it to at least 70
  • Health = Now you can raise this to 80 if you want, it will take a good deal of lvls, but be worthwhile when done
  • Agility = Pump it to 80
  • Charm = Raise to 50
  • Wisdom = Raise to 50 about the same time as charm
  • Agility = 100 (as a sneaker using fast low damage weapons, agil helps a lot)
  • Strength = You should have 80 by level 25 or earlier, depending on how you built your character.
  • Health = 95, more hps
  • Intel = 80 (more crits, ect)
  • Strength = 100 (so you can play with all the toys your warrior friends have been for the past 8 lvls)
  • Now you can pretty much do whatever you want, up intel, but don't forget charm or wis (charm moreso, it does a little bit of everything)
N on Sneaking Races

Races: Half-elf, Gnome, Dwarf, Half-orc

Pro's Vs Con's: Rangers can only use leather. You can't really make a tank out of a ranger. Although a bit harder to do, stats (like health) are higher at base lvls and can be easier if you don't want to try and bs. Also, tankly classes can be chaeaper in the exp department and will (could) end up just as well in the end if done correctly. Dwarf is a good choice as well as Kang.

Making a Tank (non sneaker)
Stat Suggestions (In order of raise)

  • LVL 1 Stats
  • Health = Get this to 80 right away
  • Agility = Try for at least 50
  • Strength = Pump it to 60 right away, shouldn't cost much as a dwarf. gnome I would put it to 60 by lvl 8 or so
  • Intel = Dwarves need not apply til at least lvl 8, gnomes could raise it to 50 at 1
  • AFTER LVL 1
  • Agility = Raise this to 65 or 70
  • Intel = Raise to 50 (if not done yet) Same with wisdom
  • Strength = Up to 80 (if a dwarf)
  • Agility = 80 
  • Health = 95 if a Dwarf, maybe even a gnome
  • Strength = 80 if not done yet
  • Intel = 65
  • Agility = 100
  • Intel = 80
  • Strength = 100
Now you can pretty much do whatever you want, charm could be upped, it helps everything.
Notes About Rangers
  • Spellcasting comes with time, stick with it and get just enough sc in the lower lvls to keep yourself casting mend and rhel ok.
  • Rhel is a godsend spell. It is what makes rangers such good scripters, no other sneakers can script (or survive) as well.
  • Pvp is something rangers excell at. They have better hps than ninjas, can bs just as well, and should have decent crits at higher lvls too. Tracking can be useful, but can be done without.
  • Rangers are expensive in terms of exp. My ranger have high exp tables, but they also makes 115k/hr at lvl 32, which not many classes could do.
  • Stick with it and have fun, resisit the reroll urge.
